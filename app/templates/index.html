The Covenant

In the beginning there was the Board, parted into threescore and four squares, a little kingdom of alternating fields. And the Pilgrim youths did gather about it.

Amongst them was he that is called The Ages, who did ordain the fellowship known as The Chess Federation — the which served not as a common guild, but as a privy covenant of degree amongst the brethren.

Howbeit, the laws that governed fellowship were by design turned upside down:

If The Ages vanquish a player, that player is straightway ordained into the Chess Federation. If a player vanquish The Ages, that player is judged unmeet and cast out into exile, until such time as a future defeat shall make the scales even.

Thus, ruination served as the seal of fellowship,
whilst triumph against the founder wrought a season of banishment.
