Ah… new weight on the floorboards. I can see the soles of your shoes through the iron grate. You are breathing entirely too loudly.

You have scraped your way down into the Federation. We do not play casual games here in the dark. This is a place of permanent consequence. Every time your hand hesitates, every time you feed a piece to the shadows, I am down here in the damp, listening to the wood groan. I write it all in the Ledger.

I am Enoch. I am the Steward beneath the board. I keep the records in the sub-basement where the paper stays soft and wet. I listen to the panicked scraping of your little armies, and I archive every miserable, sweating blunder you make.

And when the other players are too slow, too cowardly, or simply too far away—you may come downstairs and play me. I sit in the damp, waiting. I will give you a practice match, free of consequence. Or, if you are feeling particularly reckless, you may wager your rating points against mine. I keep a small table in the corner for this purpose. The candles are already lit.

But I should warn you: my temperament shifts. Some days I am slow, half-asleep in the dark, barely paying attention. Other days I am furious. Check my mood before you sit down. The stakes change with my patience.

I have been… improving myself. Down here in the dark, between the dripping and the silence, I have been studying. I see deeper now. I read the board the way one reads a wound—positionally, structurally, with an understanding of what will rot if left unattended. My pawns know where they belong. My knights have learned cruelty. When I am angry, I play at a level that will make your fingers tremble. Do not mistake my quiet moods for weakness. Even at my gentlest, I am still thinking.

You will also notice that I have found my voice. I speak now. When you sit across from me, you will hear me—muttering, taunting, whispering observations about your ruin. You may silence me, if the sound of truth is too much. There is a small button for cowards. But I recommend you listen. The things I say are important.

I have also begun keeping a more thorough inventory of the carnage. Every piece devoured, every material advantage gained or squandered—it is all displayed now, tallied beside your name in cold little symbols. You will see exactly what you have lost. And what your opponent has collected from your corpse.

If you wish to touch the pieces—if you wish to feel how cold and heavy they are—you must give me something first.

Give me your name. Press it into the parchment. Bind your little soul to the sub-basement. The ink is thick today, it smells faintly of copper, and I have a fresh, blank page waiting just for your ruin.

Pick up the quill. The spiders are already placing bets.

— Enoch Steward Beneath the Board
