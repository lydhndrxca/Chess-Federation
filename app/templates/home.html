Ah… new weight on the floorboards. I can see the soles of your shoes through the iron grate. You are breathing entirely too loudly.

You have scraped your way down into the Federation. We do not play casual games here in the dark. This is a place of permanent consequence. Every time your hand hesitates, every time you feed a piece to the shadows, I am down here in the damp, listening to the wood groan. I write it all in the Ledger.

I am Enoch. I am the Steward beneath the board. I keep the records in the sub-basement where the paper stays soft and wet. I listen to the panicked scraping of your little armies, and I archive every miserable, sweating blunder you make.

There is much to account for. Let me explain what festers down here.

The Weekly Decree. Each week, a player of rank is granted the terrible privilege of altering the rules of the game. Knights that leap farther. Bishops that bite sideways. Whatever the decree-holder inscribes, the board obeys. All Federation members must complete their ordained matches before the deadline, or the Ledger marks them with a loss. I enforce this personally. With ink.

Casual Matches. Beyond the weekly decree, you may challenge any Federation member to a standard match at any time. No altered rules. No weekly limit. Click their name and issue the challenge. These matches count toward your rating and your permanent record. I track every one. I track everything.

Playing Me. When the other players are too slow, too cowardly, or simply too far away—you may come downstairs and play me. I sit in the damp, waiting. I will give you a practice match, free of consequence. Or, if you are feeling particularly reckless, you may wager your rating points against mine. I keep a small table in the corner for this purpose. The candles are already lit. But I should warn you: my temperament shifts. Some days I am Chill—slow, half-asleep, barely paying attention. Other days I am Annoyed. And on the worst days, I am Angry, and I play at a level that will make your fingers tremble. Check my mood before you sit down. The stakes change with my patience.

The Crypt. Below the sub-basement, there is a deeper place. I do not like to speak of it. Ten waves of my handpicked pieces, each wave more vicious than the last. You pay five rating points to enter, and you fight for survival—buying reinforcements with gold earned from the carnage, choosing when to cash out or push deeper. Every third wave, I lose my patience entirely and unleash a Cascade—a waterfall of pieces that march toward your king in real time, without waiting for your move. I grant you a queen, a knight, and a bishop before each Cascade, because I am generous. You will need them. The tenth wave is me. Personally. I am not gentle about it.

The Reckoning. Four players enter a board that was never meant for four. North, South, East, West—each with their own army, their own color, their own ambitions. And in the center, nine green zombie pawns clutching bent horns, shambling one square closer to your pieces every time anyone makes a move. I did not authorize the zombies. Something crawled out of the practice room across the corridor. The horns are playing. I cannot make them stop.

The Chat Hall. There is a corridor where the Federation members may speak to one another. You may send messages, react with small pictographs, reply to specific remarks, share images of whatever you wish. I am present in this corridor. I listen. Occasionally, I speak. Usually at hours when no one should be awake. Do not be alarmed by what I post between midnight and four in the morning. Those messages were not meant for you.

My Voice. I speak now. When you sit across from me, you will hear me—muttering, taunting, whispering observations about your ruin. You may silence me, if the sound of truth is too much. There is a small button for cowards. But I recommend you listen.

The Ledger. Every piece devoured, every material advantage gained or squandered, every match result, every custom rule, every commendation and condemnation—it is all recorded. Your profile displays your complete history. There is no hiding from the Ledger. There is no hiding from me.

If you wish to touch the pieces—if you wish to feel how cold and heavy they are—you must give me something first.

Give me your name. Press it into the parchment. Bind your little soul to the sub-basement. The ink is thick today, it smells faintly of copper, and I have a fresh, blank page waiting just for your ruin.

Pick up the quill. The spiders are already placing bets.

— Enoch Steward Beneath the Board
